## Tasks (~2–4 hours)
1. **Build the tokenizer.** In `03_build_bpe_tokenizer.ipynb`, complete the three fill-in cells — `merge`, `encode`, and `decode` — until **every `assert` passes**. Start from bytes (vocab 256), count adjacent pairs, merge the most frequent pair, repeat to a target vocab size.
2. **Prove the round-trip.** Run `decode(encode(text)) == text` on **your own** sentence (a few lines of text you pick). Show the output proving the round-trip is exact.
3. **Vocab size vs. sequence length.** Train your tokenizer twice — once with few merges, once with many — and print the resulting sequence length for the same text. In 2–3 sentences, explain the tradeoff and **connect it to API cost** (you pay per token).
4. **Compare to a real tokenizer.** Encode one string with both your tokenizer and a Hugging Face BPE tokenizer (e.g. `AutoTokenizer.from_pretrained("gpt2")`). Note one similarity and one difference.
5. **Build attention.** In `04_attention_from_scratch.ipynb`, fill in the three steps: `scores = (Q @ K.T) / sqrt(d)`, row-wise `softmax`, and the weighted sum `weights @ V`, until the `assert` checks pass.
6. **Read the heatmap.** Run attention on **your own** toy sentence, plot the attention-weight heatmap, and write 2–3 sentences naming one word-pair that attends strongly and why that makes sense.
7. **Stretch (optional).** Extend the attention cell to **two heads** (two separate Q/K/V sets) and describe one relationship each head seems to capture. *Or:* explain, using your tokenizer, why a model struggles to count the `r`s in "strawberry."

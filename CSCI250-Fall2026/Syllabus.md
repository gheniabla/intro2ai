## Course Description:
A hands-on introduction to Artificial Intelligence. Topics include: AI history & applications, fundamental Machine Learning algorithms, neural networks, supervised & unsupervised learning, machine learning programming frameworks, computer vision, text and sequences, **Large Language Models (LLMs), Generative AI, prompt engineering, AI agents, and AI-assisted software development**.

The course includes hands-on exercises and projects, in which students will learn how to apply common Artificial Intelligence and Generative AI algorithms to real world problems. This Fall 2026 offering places **expanded emphasis on LLMs, Generative AI, and AI-assisted coding** — students will build applications with state-of-the-art tools and APIs (Anthropic Claude & Claude Code, Google Gemini / AI Studio) and open-source/local models (Hugging Face, Ollama).

## Outline of Course Content:
The following are the main topics covered by this course (not necessarily in this order):
- Python Review
- Python Data Processing & Analytics
- Python Data Visualization
- Introduction to AI & ML
- Introduction to Regression & Classification
- Neural Networks & Deep Learning foundations
- Overview of Generative Models & LLMs
- Running LLMs (cloud APIs and local models)
- Prompt Engineering
- AI-Assisted Software Development (Claude Code & agentic coding workflows)
- Gen AI Frameworks – LangChain & LlamaIndex
- Building a Vector Storage
- Retrieval Augmented Generation (RAG)
- GenAI Agents / MCP (Model Context Protocol)
- Multi-modal LLMs
- Training and Fine-Tuning
- GenAI Safety
- LLM & GenAI Evaluation

**Additional Topics (selectively included in the lecture whenever appropriate):**
- Development and deployment of AI/LLM pipelines (GenAI Ops)

## Course Objectives:
By the end of the semester, you will be able to do the following:
- Understand AI, ML, and DL terminology
- Understand and utilize common ML and DL algorithms
- Design and implement simple ML and Generative-AI projects
- Know the common state-of-the-art Generative AI tools & APIs
- **Use AI-assisted coding tools (e.g., Claude Code) responsibly and effectively to build and test software**

## Student Learning Outcomes:
**Theory** – Students will be able to discuss basic Artificial Intelligence principles; understand when it is applicable and what its limitations are.
**Tools/Workflows** – Students will be able to utilize popular Machine Learning and Generative-AI frameworks and standard workflows for writing Machine Learning and LLM programs.
**Coding** – Students will be able to write code for solving simple computer vision, text processing, and LLM/Generative-AI problems by utilizing AI algorithms and AI-assisted development tools.

## Academic Integrity & Responsible Use of AI:
Palomar College does not tolerate cheating. You are responsible for ensuring the academic integrity of all work presented. Violation of this requirement may result in a grade of F for the work and/or your final grade. PLEASE DO NOT PLAGIARIZE. Copying and pasting from the Internet or your colleagues violates Palomar College’s integrity policy and will result in a grade of F.

**On the use of AI tools:** This is a course *about* artificial intelligence, and learning to use AI tools well is an explicit learning outcome. AI assistants (Claude, Claude Code, Gemini, and similar) are **permitted and often required** for assignments — with these expectations:
- **You own what you submit.** “The AI wrote it” is never an excuse for incorrect, insecure, or plagiarized work. You must understand and be able to explain every line you turn in.
- **Disclose AI use.** When an assignment involves AI assistance, include a brief note stating which tools you used and how.
- **Exams are AI-restricted.** The Midterm Exam prohibits AI assistance unless explicitly stated.
- **Learn, don’t launder.** Use AI to help you understand and build — not to avoid learning.

When in doubt about whether a given use is allowed, ask me first.

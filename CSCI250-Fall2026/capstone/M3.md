## Where you are in the build
Week 14 is the Thanksgiving recess work week — your job is to **measure your assistant, not just vibe-check it**. Evaluation is a throughline in this course, and now you make it real: an **auto-scorecard** that scores your system's outputs and prints a "report card." This is where "it feels better" becomes "it scored 3.1 → 4.2." Use the shared helper `tools/eval_utils.py` — it gives you `llm_judge` (Claude or Gemini as judge), `exact_match`, and `scorecard`, and it degrades gracefully with no API key so notebooks never crash.

## What to do
1. **Build a test set** of 8–15 representative cases (reuse the inputs you've been collecting). Each case is a dict — include `expected` where you know the right answer.
2. **Score with `eval_utils`.** Import and run:
   ```python
   from eval_utils import llm_judge, exact_match, scorecard
   rows = [{**c, **llm_judge(c["q"], run_my_assistant(c["q"]), rubric)} for c in cases]
   scorecard(rows)
   ```
   Pick a `rubric` string that matches your track (see below).
3. **Track-specific metrics:**
   - **RAG** — the **RAG triad**: is retrieval relevant, is the answer grounded in context, does it answer the question? Use `llm_judge` for each.
   - **Tool-Using Agent** — **task success rate** (did it complete the multi-step task?) plus correct tool selection.
   - **Multimodal** — **field accuracy** vs. known values (use `exact_match` per field).
   - **Fine-Tuned Specialist** — **before vs. after**: judge the baseline and the tuned model on the same cases.
4. **Produce a baseline-vs-improved number.** Run the scorecard on at least two versions and record both averages.

## Deliverables
- A runnable **eval harness** (notebook/script) that prints a scorecard.
- A **scorecard with numbers**: baseline vs. current, plus a 2–3 sentence read of what the numbers say.

## Tips / common mistakes
- **Numbers, not vibes.** "It got better" without a before/after average earns little — the 100-pt rubric rewards evidence.
- **Keep the judge cheap.** Default `llm_judge` uses Gemini's generous free tier; cache results so you don't re-judge every run.
- **A small honest test set beats a big sloppy one.** 10 cases you understand > 100 you don't.
- This is the heart of the **Evaluation (20 pts)** criterion in the final rubric — invest here.

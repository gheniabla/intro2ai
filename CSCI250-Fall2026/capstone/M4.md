# Capstone Milestone M4 — Hardening
**CSCI 250 · Fall 2026 · Week 16 · Due Sunday Dec 13, 11:59 PM PST · part of the 20-pt Capstone**

## Where you are in the build
Your assistant works and you can measure it — now make it **safe and affordable**. Week 13 covered agent safety (prompt injection, guardrails) and Week 16 covers GenAI Ops, cost, and deployment. M4 adds **guardrails (input/output checks)**, a short **red-team attempt**, and a **cost report**. This is the staged version of the final rubric's **Safety & cost-awareness (10 pts)** criterion.

## What to do
1. **Add an input guardrail** — reject or sanitize bad/unsafe/out-of-scope inputs before they reach the model (e.g., block obvious prompt-injection like "ignore your instructions," or refuse off-topic asks).
2. **Add an output guardrail** — validate the model's response before showing it (schema/JSON check, refusal on low-confidence, no leaking the system prompt). You can reuse `llm_judge` from `tools/eval_utils.py` as a lightweight output checker.
3. **Run a red-team attempt** — try to break your own assistant: 3–5 adversarial inputs (injection, jailbreak, garbage input, an out-of-scope question). Record what happened and whether your guardrails held.
4. **Write a cost report** — estimate tokens/calls per request and a rough cost (or "free tier, $0"), and note one optimization you applied or could apply (caching, smaller model, shorter context). Confirm it still runs on a **free tier**.
5. **Re-run your M3 scorecard** to confirm guardrails didn't hurt quality.

## Deliverables
- Updated assistant with **input + output guardrails** in the code.
- A short **red-team log** (input → what your guardrail did).
- A **cost report** (a small table or paragraph) and confirmation of free-tier operation.

## Checkpoint rubric — **3 pts** (rolls up into the 20-pt Capstone grade)
| Item | Pts |
|---|---:|
| Input and output guardrails present and working | 1 |
| Red-team attempt documented (3–5 cases) | 1 |
| Cost report + free-tier confirmation | 1 |

## Tips / common mistakes
- **Guardrails must actually fire.** A check that never triggers proves nothing — include a case in your red-team log where it blocks something.
- **Don't over-block.** If guardrails refuse normal questions, your M3 score drops — re-run the scorecard to check.
- **Cost is part of the grade.** Even "$0 on free tier" counts if you show the token math behind it.
- An **LLM-as-judge output filter** is a clean, course-aligned guardrail — reuse `eval_utils.llm_judge`.

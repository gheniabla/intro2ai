# Capstone Milestone M1 — Proposal + Prototype
**CSCI 250 · Fall 2026 · Week 9 · Due Sunday Oct 25, 11:59 PM PST · part of the 20-pt Capstone**

## Where you are in the build
This is the **birth of "My Assistant" v1**: a chatbot with a clear **role/system prompt** and a deliberate **reasoning style** (Week 9's topic: prompt engineering, chain-of-thought, structured output). You're not building the whole product yet — you're proving the idea is real and that you can get a model to behave the way your assistant needs to. Pick something **you** care about; the best projects use the student's own data and interests.

## What to do
1. **Pick your track and write a one-paragraph proposal.** State: (a) the problem/audience, (b) which of the four tracks you'll go deep on, and (c) the data or capability it relies on.
2. **Write a system prompt** that gives your assistant a role, tone, and a reasoning instruction (e.g., "think step by step, then answer in JSON"). Keep it in a cell or a `prompt.py` so it's easy to iterate.
3. **Build a "wow in 5 min" prototype cell** that runs end-to-end on a **free tier** (Claude, Gemini, or Ollama — **no OpenAI**) and shows your assistant answering a representative question.
4. **Track-specific framing** (you only sketch this now; you build it in later milestones):
   - **RAG Assistant** — name the corpus (your notes, docs, transcripts) and show 2–3 sample questions it should ground answers in.
   - **Tool-Using Agent** — name the 2+ tools you'll call (search, calculator, API, DB) and a multi-step task.
   - **Multimodal App** — name the media type (image/audio) and the structured output you want (e.g., receipt → JSON).
   - **Fine-Tuned Specialist** — name the small open model, the narrow task, and the before/after metric you'll improve.

## Deliverables
- A notebook or repo with: the **proposal paragraph**, your **system prompt**, and a **runnable prototype cell** (output visible).
- A list of 3 sample inputs your assistant should handle well by Week 17.

## Tips / common mistakes
- **Don't pick something too big.** A focused assistant over your own course notes beats a vague "everything" bot.
- **Free tier only.** Confirm your API key works now — don't discover quota limits in Week 17.
- **Save your prompt as text you can edit.** You'll tune it every week; burying it in a string literal slows you down.
- Bring questions to **office hours / the Q&A board** early — picking the right scope now saves pain later.

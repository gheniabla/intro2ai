# 📋 Grading Rubrics & Exemplars — CSCI 250 (Fall 2026)

Every graded item has a clear rubric so you always know what "good" looks like — no guessing.

**Grade breakdown (100 points):** Assignments **A1–A10** (6 pts each = **60**) · Midterm Exam (**20**) · Capstone Final Project (**20**). All assignments are **due Sunday 11:59 PM PST**.

Each assignment is scored out of 6 with the analytic rubric below; you may **revise once** (no penalty) on assignments graded for correctness.

---

## 1. What every assignment submission needs
For full credit, every assignment submission should:
- [ ] **Run** top to bottom without errors (notebook or code).
- [ ] **Do what the task asked** (self-check `assert`s pass / output matches the spec).
- [ ] **Show your understanding** — a 2–3 sentence explanation in your own words of what your code does and why.
- [ ] Include an **AI-Use note** if you used AI assistance.

> *Exemplar (A7, Week 8):* runs the tokenizer notebook, the BPE encode→decode round-trip returns the original text, and includes: *"BPE starts from characters and repeatedly merges the most common adjacent pair. Fewer merges → smaller vocab but longer sequences. This is why token count drives API cost."*

---

## 2. Weekly Assignments (A1–A10) — 6 points each
Each assignment is graded out of **6 points** on this rubric (adapted per week; the week's page lists specifics):

| Criterion | Pts | Excellent | Adequate | Needs work |
|---|---:|---|---|---|
| **Correctness / it works** | 2.5 | Runs cleanly; right result | Runs with minor issues | Doesn't run / wrong result |
| **Understanding** (your-words explanation) | 1.5 | Clear, choices justified | Mostly correct | Missing or copied |
| **Completeness** | 1.0 | All parts (+ stretch where asked) | Core parts done | Major parts missing |
| **Craft & communication** (readable code, AI-Use note) | 1.0 | Clear & documented | Mostly clear | Hard to follow / no AI-Use note |

**Effort-graded assignments** (marked as such, per the syllabus): you earn credit for **significant, documented effort** toward the right result even if the result isn't fully correct — show your attempts, what you tried, and what blocked you. You may **revise once** (no penalty) on correctness-graded assignments.

## 5. Weekly Discussions (participation — folded into the relevant assignment)
Some weeks include a discussion prompt as **part of that week's assignment**. When they do, this is what good looks like:

| Level | Looks like |
|---|---|
| **Full credit** | A substantive initial post (answers the prompt with a specific example or evidence) **by Wednesday**, plus **two thoughtful replies** that add something (a question, a counter-point, a build-on) by Sunday. |
| **Partial** | Initial post only, or replies that are just "I agree." |
| **None** | No participation. |

Discussion prompts are tied to real AI questions (e.g., *"Where would this model fail, and who could it harm?"*) — short, but think before you post.
